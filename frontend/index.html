The robot that pulls servers so you don't have to.

An autonomous rack-service robot. It finds the failing board, raises to its slot, latches on, and rolls it out on its rails — repeatably, in one motion.

How it works

  1. 01

    Identify

    Vision picks out the target server; the digital twin tracks its rack position.

  2. 02

    Elevate

    A vertical elevator lifts the arm to the exact shelf height.

  3. 03

    Extend & latch

    A horizontal arm extends forward; a perpendicular gripper engages the chassis.

  4. 04

    Retract

    Controlled pull along the rails, back to a drop-off position at the base.

Pick a server. We'll fetch it.

Click any slot in the rack. The elevator rises, the arm extends, and the board slides out. This is the same loop the real dashboard runs against the Pi.

What the software does

Digital twin

Live model of rack state, slot occupancy, and history.

Vision ID

Per-slot identification and automatic failure detection.

Job queue

Autonomous task scheduler with multi-step pipelines.

Self-calibration

Smart alignment so the arm meets the board every time.

Telemetry

Real-time streams: position, load, camera feed.

Replay

Action logs you can scrub, diff, and replay.

Predictive maintenance

Flag servers likely to need service next.

What-if mode

Simulate the motion before the robot commits.

Fault recovery

Detect, back off, retry — without a human in the loop.
